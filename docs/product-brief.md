# Backstory Product Brief

## One-Sentence Definition

Backstory is a personalized short-form media feed that uses a student's current
coursework to surface entertaining context, stories, and explanations around what
they are already learning.

## Positioning

Backstory should feel closer to opening a consumer content app than opening an
education app. The student does not arrive to study, complete work, or prove mastery.
They arrive to scroll. Because the feed understands their classes, passive time in the
app builds familiarity with the people, ideas, periods, vocabulary, and relationships
surrounding their coursework.

The product is not "TikTok with quizzes." Its differentiator is a curriculum-aware
content graph and feed that can combine relevant human creativity with precise,
spoiler-aware AI generation.

## Problem

Students spend significant time with short-form media, while school material is often
confined to formal assignments and classroom presentation. Existing education
products commonly ask students to begin another intentional study session. Existing
consumer feeds generally do not understand where a student is in a course.

Backstory bridges those environments. It turns the LMS into a relevance signal rather
than another task list.

## Target User

The initial target is a secondary-school student who uses an LMS for daily coursework
and already consumes short-form content after school. The student may be curious, bored,
or simply passing time. The experience must still work when they have no explicit
intention to learn.

## Core Experience

The student opens directly into a vertical feed. Posts may include:

- Human creator videos relevant to the current topic
- AI-narrated visual stories
- Character, event, or concept backstories
- Historical and cultural context
- Text-message or social-post reinterpretations
- Visual maps, diagrams, timelines, and comparisons
- Memes and point-of-view scenes
- Details to notice that do not spoil upcoming material
- Optional opinion polls without grades or correct-answer treatment

For *The Great Gatsby* chapters 4-5, examples include:

- Why Gatsby hosts large parties while remaining socially distant
- A spoiler-safe relationship map
- The cultural meaning of new money and old money in the 1920s
- A gossip-column version of what the student already knows about Gatsby
- What to notice about the chapter 5 reunion
- A creator's explanation of the green light as a recurring symbol

## Personalization

Backstory combines learning-context signals with ordinary feed behavior.

Learning-context signals:

- Active courses and modules
- Current reading or chapter position
- Upcoming topics and due dates
- Teacher-provided resources
- Completed or unlocked module items

Passive preference signals:

- Watch duration and completion
- Replays and pauses
- Saves and shares
- Skips
- "More like this" and "less like this"
- Creator, format, and subject affinity

These signals should adjust content selection invisibly. Do not expose mastery labels or
tell a student that their behavior proves they are weak in a subject.

## Trust

Each post should make its origin legible without overwhelming the experience:

- Human-created or AI-created label
- Creator or source identity
- Course connection available through "why this?"
- Source material available when appropriate
- Spoiler boundary derived from the current learning context

Generated content must be grounded in available sources and should distinguish fact,
interpretation, and creative framing.

## MVP Success

The hackathon MVP succeeds when a judge can see that:

1. The feed is enjoyable and coherent as a consumer product.
2. It changes based on a real-looking LMS state rather than a hard-coded topic label.
3. GPT-5.6 materially interprets course context and creates grounded content plans.
4. Human and AI content appear together with clear provenance.
5. The system prevents spoilers and explains recommendation relevance.
6. Passive interaction changes subsequent recommendations.
7. No required academic task interrupts the scrolling experience.

## Future Product Surface

Potential future capabilities include real LMS authorization, teacher or district
controls, creator submission and compensation, richer multimodal generation, content
licensing partnerships, guardian controls, and institution-level safety management.
They should not expand the hackathon scope until the core feed is working.
